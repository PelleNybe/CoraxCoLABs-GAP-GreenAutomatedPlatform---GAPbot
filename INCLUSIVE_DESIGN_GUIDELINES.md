# Inclusive Design Guidelines (2026 HRI Standards)

Corax CoLAB is committed to inclusive design in all our robotic and software interfaces, ensuring our systems are accessible, understandable, and ergonomic for diverse human operators.

1.  **Cognitive Load Management**: Interfaces must prioritize critical information and use progressive disclosure to avoid overwhelming users, especially in high-stress emergency response scenarios.
2.  **Color Universal Design**: All status indicators and mapping overlays must be distinguishable without relying solely on color hue (e.g., combining color with distinct shapes, patterns, or textual labels).
3.  **Physical Ergonomics**: Hardware control stations and manual overrides must accommodate a wide range of physical abilities and statures.
4.  **Transparent AI Actionability**: The rationale behind autonomous decisions (e.g., why the system altered a trajectory) must be queryable and presented in plain language.
5.  **Language and Localization**: System feedback and mission planning interfaces should support multiple languages and avoid culturally specific idioms that could cause misinterpretation.
